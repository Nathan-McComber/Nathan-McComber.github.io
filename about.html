About Me

I'm a 3rd year UX Design student at the Rochester Institute of Technology. It took me a while to decide on UX Design as an area of study, but some key things that drew me to UX Design are:

  • I enjoy being creative
  • I want to help people and actually affect their lives
  • I enjoy working on things that people interact with

My Journey to UX Design

I've loved creating things for as long as I can remember, and I've enjoyed experiencing stories through video games for most of my life. From a young age I intended to be a game developer, and I even enrolled into the Game Design and Development program at the Rochester Institute of Technology. However, I soon realized that game development itself was not what I wanted to do. I wasn't super interested in algorithms, physics, or graphics rendering, I was interested in game mechanics, level design choices, and artistic decisions. Essentially, I was interested in the aspects of game development that delivered a positive experience to the player.

After coming to this realization, I pivoted to UX Design and have strove to learn about providing positive experiences through apps and products ever since.

Other Interests

When I'm not doing UX Design, I enjoy drawing, writing, reading, and playing the occasional video game. While I haven't had any formal art education, I love sketching and have taught myself through YouTube videos and How-to-Draw books. Typically, I enjoy drawing creatures and writing up backstories/lore for them. I also do a little bit of character design and want to push myself by drawing landscapes and architecture. My current art goals are:

  • Learn to draw with a variety of pencil hardnesses
  • Add color to my art with watercolor paints
  • Get comfortable with drawing animals/people in an environment (not just floating in space)

I also like writing my own stories and am currently handwriting a small fantasy story in a little notebook. In the future, I hope to draw scenes from the story or use stop motion animation to bring the tale to life.
