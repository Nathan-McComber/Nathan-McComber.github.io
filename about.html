About Me

I'm a 3rd year UX Design student at the Rochester Institute of Technology. It took me a while to decide on UX Design as an area of study, but some key things that drew me to UX Design are:

  • I enjoy being creative
  • I want to help people and actually affect their lives
  • I enjoy working on things that people interact with

My Journey to UX Design

My love for being creative started when I was around 3 or 4 years old. At that age, LEGOs were my life and I made original builds with them every day. If there was a toy that I wanted but didn't have, I would build my own LEGO version of it. Building with LEGOs allowed me to turn ideas into physical objects and helped me to expand my imaginative capabilities.

Then, at around the age of 6, I played The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time. It opened up a whole new world of possibilities to me, and I felt that the game had a living, breathing world inside of it. The experience and feelings the game gave me stuck with me, and I realized that being able to interact with a product could make it much more impactful.

From that point forward, I thought that I wanted to get into game development. I took coding classes and followed YouTube tutorials in an attempt to make my own game. I even went as far as enrolling into the Game Design and Development program at the Rochester Institute of Technology. However, I soon realized that game development was not what I wanted to do. I wasn't interested in algorithms, physics, or graphics rendering, I was interested in game mechanics, level design choices, and artistic decisions. Essentially, I was interested in the aspects of game development that delivered an experience to the player.

I then pivoted to UX Design and strove to learn about how to provide experiences through apps and products.

Other Interests

When I'm not doing UX Design, I enjoy drawing, writing, reading, and playing the occasional video game. While I haven't had any formal art education, I love sketching and have taught myself through YouTube videos and How-to-Draw books. Typically, I enjoy drawing creatures and writing up backstories/lore for them. I also do a little bit of character design and want to push myself by drawing landscapes and architecture. My current art goals are:

  • Learn to draw with a variety of pencil hardnesses
  • Add color to my art with watercolor paints
  • Get comfortable with drawing animals/people in an environment (not just floating in space)

I also like writing my own stories and am currently handwriting a small fantasy story in a little notebook. In the future, I hope to draw scenes from the story or use stop motion animation to bring the tale to life.
